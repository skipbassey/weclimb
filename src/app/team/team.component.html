Team
Avery Rosser, LPC

Founder, We Climb Counseling and Consulting LLC. Avery earned a Master of Science degree in Community Counseling as well as a Post Masters of School Counseling from Columbus State University. He has completed advanced training in Collaborative Couples Training, Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR), Motivational Interviewing (MI), and Parent Management Training (PMT). Avery is a Licensed Professional Counselor, Board Certified Counselor and Certified School Counselor in the State of Georgia.

Avery has over six years of counseling experience with a wide range of clients in a variety of treatment settings. These have included providing counseling in a psychiatric hospital, public education system K-12, inpatient, and partial hospital settings, and currently with the US Department of Veterans Affairs as well as his own outpatient practice. He also is a part of his church counseling department.

Avery has a broad and unique base of experience counseling children and adolescents. Areas of emphasis with children include mood disorders, posttraumatic stress disorder, conduct disorder, and attention deficit hyperactive disorder. Avery also has significant experience working with children and adolescents who have experienced developmental delays, varying degrees of trauma, various types of physical and/or sexual abuse, abandonment and/or neglect, as well as dysfunctional family systems.

Keshara Rosser, MSW

Co-Founder, We Climb Counseling & Consulting LLC. Keshara earned a Master of Social Work from the University of Alabama. Keshara completed her internship experience at Georgia Center for Child Advocacy where she served as forensic intern and family advocate to ensure that a child's non-offending caregivers feel supported during the investigation process. Keshara completed her practicum experience with Pathways Transition Program where she conducted outpatient school-based counseling. Keshara's area of focus is working with adolescents, teens, young adults, and families that struggle with anxiety, depression and trauma.
